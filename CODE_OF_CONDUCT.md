# Code of Conduct

SparseLab is a community scaffolding project for sparse-from-scratch
machine learning research. We want it to be a welcoming, technically
rigorous place to contribute. This document sets the expectations.

## In short

Be direct. Be kind. Show your work. Credit your sources.

## What we expect from everyone

- **Focus on the technical substance.** The best version of a
  disagreement on this project is two people pointing at a benchmark,
  a paper, or a line of code. The worst version is two people arguing
  about tone.
- **Cite your claims.** When you report a number, make it
  reproducible. When you cite prior work, link the paper or the
  repo. "Trust me" is not a reviewable claim.
- **Credit the community.** Most of what we do builds on other
  people's work. Cerebras, Neural Magic, rigl-torch, the DST paper
  authors, the PyTorch team — credit them in docs and docstrings.
- **Assume good faith in reviewers.** Reviewers ask for changes
  because they want the PR to land well, not because they dislike
  you. If a request feels confusing, ask for specifics rather than
  taking it personally.
- **Assume learning in contributors.** Contributors are not assumed
  to already know everything. Explain why a change is needed — our
  "teach while we build" principle applies to reviews too.

## What we won't accept

- Personal attacks, harassment, or discrimination against anyone based
  on who they are.
- Dismissive framing ("this is obviously wrong", "did you even
  read…") without pointing at the actual problem.
- Deliberate misrepresentation of benchmarks, reproducibility, or
  attribution. Honest mistakes are fine and we all make them;
  deliberate bad numbers are not.
- Publishing another person's private information without consent.
- Low-effort drive-by contributions masquerading as substantive work
  (AI-generated PRs that don't compile, "I can have this ready in 3
  days" comments with no follow-through, etc.). We'd rather have one
  real PR than five fake ones.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- The SparseLab GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, review
  comments)
- Any official community channel (Discord, mailing list, etc., if
  and when they exist)
- Public representation of the project at conferences, blog posts,
  or social media when speaking on the project's behalf

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of
Conduct, email the maintainer directly:
[darshanfofadiya@gmail.com](mailto:darshanfofadiya@gmail.com).

Reports will be read, taken seriously, and responded to. We will
protect reporter anonymity to the extent possible.

## Enforcement

Depending on severity, consequences for unacceptable behavior may
include:

- A private note from the maintainer asking you to change course
- A public correction on the relevant thread
- Temporary removal from project channels
- Permanent ban from the project

The maintainer has final discretion. We aim for proportionality — a
first-time heated comment gets a conversation, not a ban.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
but is deliberately shorter and more project-specific. We kept the
principles and dropped the boilerplate.

TL;DR, for hurried readers: be direct, be kind, cite your work.

contributions are not.
